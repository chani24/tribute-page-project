FELA

A LEGEND


The story of an Afrobeat legend.

Fela Anikulapo Kuti was born on 15 October 1938. He was a Nigerian multi-instrumentalist, musician, composer, the pioneer of Afrobeat music, human right activist, and political nonconformist. Popularly known as Fela, he, the cousin of popular Nobel laureate Wole Syinka, was born into the Ransom-kuti family. He attended the Abeokuta Grammar School in Abeokuta and later went to London in 1958 and decided to study music at the Trinity College of Music. His main instrument was the saxophone.

Fela formed his first band and married in 1960 and married his first wife Remi Taylor in 1963. Fela moved back to africa to continue his music and in 1969, Fela named his music, Afrobeat. In the 70s Fela's music and political views began to change. He used his music to critisize the ruling government. He released an album, Zombie which described the methods of the Nigerian army. It resulted in attacks on his kalakuta republic and the communes by the government which in turn led to the death of his mother. He further infuriated the government by releasing more songs like Coffin For Head of State and unknown soldier. His music continued to spread and he gained influence. He was arrested and beaten on several occasions, he slowed down music production and release and it was then rumoured he was ill. on the 3rd August 1997, his brother, Olikoye Ransom-Kuti, announced his death from Kaposi's sarcoma. A result of AIDS he suffered.

"Music is the weapon of the future"-Fela Anikulapo Kuti.
